Overview

1. What is Anatomy & Physiology?

Anatomy: Study of structure of the body. Example: Heart has 4 chambers.

Physiology: Study of function of the body. Example: How heart pumps blood.

Simple: Anatomy = Structure, Physiology = Function

2. Levels of Organisation

The body is organized in levels like a system:

Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ System → Organism

Cell: Smallest unit of life

Tissue: Group of similar cells

Organ: Different tissues working together

Organ System: Organs working together

Organism: Whole human body

3. Types of Tissues

Epithelial Tissue: Covers body surface, protection and absorption

Connective Tissue: Supports and connects (blood, bone)

Muscle Tissue: Helps in movement (skeletal, smooth, cardiac)

Nervous Tissue: Controls body, made of neurons

4. Organ Systems

Digestive System: Breaks food

Respiratory System: Breathing

Circulatory System: Blood transport

Nervous System: Control and coordination

Muscular System: Movement

Skeletal System: Support

Excretory System: Waste removal

Reproductive System: Reproduction

5. Body Positions and Planes

Anatomical Position: Standing straight, hands at sides, palms forward

Sagittal Plane: Divides body into left and right

Frontal Plane: Divides body into front and back

Transverse Plane: Divides body into upper and lower

6. Basic Life Processes

Metabolism: All chemical reactions

Respiration: Energy production

Digestion: Breakdown of food

Circulation: Transport of substances

Excretion: Removal of waste

7. Homeostasis

The body maintains a constant internal environment.

Example: Body temperature and blood sugar control.

If homeostasis fails, disease occurs.

Pro-Level Understanding

Cells = Employees

Tissues = Teams

Organs = Departments

Systems = Company

Nervous system = Fast control

Endocrine system = Slow control

Exam Strategy

Focus on tissue types, organ systems, and levels of organization.

Common mistakes: Confusing tissue types and muscle types.
